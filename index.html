                 if thou must love me
                                        -Elizabeth Barrett Browning

if thou must love me, let it be for nought

except for love's sake only.

do not say,

"I love her for her smile-

her look—

her way

of speaking gently,

for a trick of thought
that falls in well with mine,
and certes brought

a sense of pleasant ease
on such a day"

for these themselves, Beloved,
may be changed, or change for thee -
and love, so wrought...

...may be unwrought so!

neither love me
for thine own dear pity’s:

wiping my cheeks dry:
a creature might forget to weep,
who bore thy comfort long,

and lose thy love thereby!

but love me for love's sake, that evermore

thou mayst love on, through love's eternity.
